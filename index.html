  • What is an NFT??

    At the most basic level, non-exchangeable tokens are unique, verifiable digital assets that can be exchanged between creators and buyers.

    These unique characteristics are important in distinguishing NFTs from other digital assets; one NFT is not interchangeable for another, such as banknotes or stocks, but is unique and has its own value.

  • What makes an NFT valuable?

    The value of an NFT comes from the property it represents, which is generally something that exists in the digital world such as an original work of art or digital memorabilia.

    The NFT itself does not necessarily contain a digital property, but points to its location on the blockchain.

    Like a concert ticket or a deed to physical property, an NFT reflects the value of the thing it represents.

  • What's the connection between NFTs and cryptocurrency?

    NFT is not a cryptocurrency, but is created using technology similar to Ethereum and Bitcoin.

    Also, like cryptocurrencies, NFTs exist on the blockchain, which verifies their unique identity and ownership.

    The blockchain also keeps a record of all transactions connected to the NFT and the properties they represent.

    Many NFTs are stored on the Ethereum blockchain.

  • Are NFTs the future of art and collectibles?

    It depends on who you ask.

    Artists, musicians, athletes, celebrities, and more find NFT attractive because it offers new and unique ways to sell their wares — including things like GIFs, memes, and tweets — directly to fans.

    NFT also gives artists the opportunity to program in continued royalties if sold to new owners.

    The gallery sees the potential to reach a new generation of collectors.

  • What questions remain about NFTs?

    many.

    Although NFT technology has been around since 2014, it is only just starting to be tested in the real world.

    Because NFTs run on the blockchain, they can offer potential uses outside of art and collectibles.

    Suggested uses range from video games and fashion, to vehicle ownership and real estate.

    In theory, NFT can offer efficiency and security through embedded smart contracts.

    However, the NFT market is still very new, and questions about rights, ownership, intellectual property and more have not been fully explored.
